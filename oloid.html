Oloid Internship

Reflecting on my time as a product design intern at Oloid, a SaaS company working on modernizing access control systems

Duration

June - September 2022

Role

Product Design Intern

Tools

Figma, Adobe XD, Photoshop

Context

During my time at Oloid, I designed web and mobile interfaces for their cloud identity management portal. I also helped establish the company's new brand guidelines across new UI screens and marketing materials.

If you would like to learn more about my work at Oloid, please feel free to email me.

What I Learned

Take initiative

As the only designer on the team, it was up to me to determine my own design process. I learned that I had to be self-motivated and take the initiative to explore and experiment with different design methods until I found what worked best for me.

This realization was both empowering and intimidating. On the one hand, it gave me the freedom to be creative and find my own way. On the other hand, it meant that I was solely responsible for the success or failure of my design work. This was a big responsibility, but it also showed me that the I was trusted to take ownership of my work and make important decisions.

Communication is essential

While my design process was primarily for my own benefit, it was important to communicate my process and insights to others. As the only designer on the team, I had to work closely with other departments, and I realized that they didn't necessarily care about the specifics of my design process. However, they did care about the end result and how my design decisions would impact the product and users.

This taught me the importance of being able to distill my design process and insights into actionable recommendations that others can understand and act on.

Ask questions

Finally, I learned that it's important to reach out to my coworkers for help and support when I need it. At first, I was too nervous to ask for help and set up meetings. However, I eventually realized that my colleagues were there to support me and help me succeed. This was an important lesson for me to learn - I am not expected to know everything, and it's okay to ask questions. In fact, it's often the best way to learn.
